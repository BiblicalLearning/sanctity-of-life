Life Begins at Conception

God knows us before we're born.

"For you created my inmost being; you knit me together in my mother's womb. I praise you because I am fearfully and wonderfully made; your works are wonderful, I know that full well."

‚Äî Jeremiah 1:5

‚Äî Psalm 139:16

Even when "unformed," God sees us. He has already written our days. Life doesn't begin at birth - it begins when God begins His work.

Part Three

What Abortion Really Is

Speaking truth with compassion.

If life begins at conception, and all human life bears God's image, then abortion is the taking of an innocent human life.

"You shall not murder."

‚Äî Exodus 20:13

The Hebrew word used here is "ratsach" - the intentional killing of an innocent person. This is distinct from killing in self-defense or just war.

"There are six things the Lord hates, seven that are detestable to him: haughty eyes, a lying tongue, hands that shed innocent blood..."

‚Äî Proverbs 6:16-17

The Hard Truth

God specifically lists "hands that shed innocent blood" among the things He hates. There is no blood more innocent than that of an unborn child who has done nothing wrong.

What About Exodus 21:22-25?

Some claim this passage shows unborn life has less value. Let's look carefully:

"If people are fighting and hit a pregnant woman and she gives birth prematurely but there is no serious injury, the offender must be fined... But if there is serious injury, you are to take life for life, eye for eye..."

‚Äî Exodus 21:22-25

This passage actually protects the unborn. If the child is harmed, the penalty is "life for life" - the same as for any other person. The unborn child's life is valued equally.

A Note of Compassion

If you've had an abortion, this truth may be painful to read. Please know that acknowledging the truth is the first step toward healing. God's grace is greater than any sin. Keep reading - healing is coming.

Part Four

The World's Arguments

Examining common justifications biblically.

"My Body, My Choice"

"Do you not know that your bodies are temples of the Holy Spirit, who is in you, whom you have received from God? You are not your own; you were bought at a price."

‚Äî 1 Corinthians 6:19-20

Scripture teaches we are not our own. We belong to God. But more importantly, the baby is not the mother's body - it's a separate human being with distinct DNA, often a different blood type, and its own heartbeat.

"It's Just a Clump of Cells"

Science confirms what Scripture declares:

  • üß¨Unique DNA from conception - not the mother's body
  • üíìHeartbeat detectable at 6 weeks
  • üß†Brain waves at 6-7 weeks
  • üëãResponds to touch at 8 weeks

"What About Rape or Incest?"

These situations are tragic and the victims deserve compassion and support. However:

Two Wrongs Don't Make a Right

The child conceived in rape is innocent. Punishing the child for the father's crime doesn't bring justice - it creates a second victim. Many women who've had abortions after rape report that the abortion added trauma rather than bringing healing.

"What About the Life of the Mother?"

When a mother's life is genuinely at risk, the goal should be to save both lives. In rare cases where this isn't possible, this becomes a tragic medical decision - not an elective abortion. These cases are extremely rare and should not be used to justify abortion on demand.

"Abortion Helps Women"

The Hidden Trauma

Studies show many women experience depression, anxiety, guilt, and PTSD after abortion. The abortion industry profits while women suffer in silence. True help means supporting women through pregnancy and beyond - not offering a "quick fix" that often leads to lasting pain.

Healing and Forgiveness

There is hope. There is healing. There is grace.

To Those Who've Had Abortions

If you've had an abortion, please read this section carefully. God loves you. He wants to heal you. You are not beyond His reach. The enemy wants you to stay in shame - but Jesus came to set you free.

"If we confess our sins, he is faithful and just and will forgive us our sins and purify us from all unrighteousness."

‚Äî 1 John 1:9

"All unrighteousness" - that includes abortion. There is no sin beyond God's forgiveness for those who come to Him in repentance.

"Therefore, there is now no condemnation for those who are in Christ Jesus."

‚Äî Romans 8:1

If you have confessed and repented, you are not condemned. The enemy may accuse you, but God has declared you forgiven.

"Come now, let us settle the matter," says the Lord. "Though your sins are like scarlet, they shall be as white as snow; though they are red as crimson, they shall be like wool."

‚Äî Isaiah 1:18
